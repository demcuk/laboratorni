leading (line-height)

%

What is called line-height in CSS is also known as leading in graphic design. The name comes from the thin strips of lead that typesetters would put between lines of type to increase vertical distance. Line-height between 120% and 150% is best for legibility. Anything less and the lines can overlap. Anything more and the lines are too far apart to read quickly.
